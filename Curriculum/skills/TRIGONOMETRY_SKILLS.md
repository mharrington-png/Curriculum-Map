# Trigonometry Supporting Skills

This is the initial reusable skill library extracted from Math 32. Skills are smaller than learning objectives and may support objectives in Math 31, Math 32, Math 49, and later courses.

## Reused Algebra and Function Skills

Math 32 reuses canonical skills defined in the earlier algebra libraries rather than redefining them here:

- Linear-equation solving: `SK-ALG-INVERSE-OPERATIONS`, `SK-ALG-VARIABLE-ISOLATE`
- Quadratic solving: `SK-QUAD-METHOD-SELECT` and its method-specific supporting skills
- Factoring: `SK-FACTOR-GCF`, `SK-FACTOR-TRINOMIAL-MONIC`, `SK-FACTOR-TRINOMIAL-GENERAL`, `SK-FACTOR-DIFF-SQUARES`
- Radical simplification: `SK-RAD-FACTOR-PERFECT`
- Fraction operations: `SK-NUM-FRACTION-OPERATE` and the rational-expression skills
- Function evaluation: `SK-FUNC-INPUT-OUTPUT`, `SK-FUNC-SOLVE-INPUT`
- Inverse evaluation: `SK-FUNC-INVERSE-SWAP`, `SK-FUNC-INVERSE-ALGEBRA`
- Transformations: `SK-FUNC-TRANSFORM-HORIZONTAL`, `SK-FUNC-TRANSFORM-VERTICAL`, `SK-FUNC-TRANSFORM-SCALE`
- Graph features: the feature-specific linear, quadratic, exponential, and logarithmic skills

## Angle and Triangle Skills

- **SK-TRIG-NOTATION-VOCAB:** Recall the six trigonometric functions, their reciprocal relationships, and their notation.
- **SK-TRIG-ANGLE-CONVERT:** Convert between degree and radian measure.
- **SK-TRIG-ANGLE-COTERMINAL:** Generate and recognize coterminal angles.
- **SK-TRIG-ANGLE-REFERENCE:** Determine a reference angle and its quadrant.
- **SK-TRIG-TRIANGLE-LABEL:** Label sides relative to a selected angle.
- `SK-TRI-SPECIAL-RATIOS`, `SK-TRI-PYTHAGOREAN`, and `SK-TRI-TRIG-RATIO` are reused from the geometry library.
- **SK-TRIG-TRIANGLE-DATA:** Classify triangle data as SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, or SSA.
- **SK-TRIG-AMBIGUOUS-CASE:** Test and interpret the possible outcomes of SSA data.
- **SK-TRIG-TRIANGLE-AREA-SAS:** Calculate triangle area from two sides and the included angle.
- **SK-TRIG-LAW-SINES:** Apply the Law of Sines to determine missing sides or angles.
- **SK-TRIG-LAW-COSINES:** Apply the Law of Cosines to determine missing sides or angles.
- **SK-TRIG-TRIANGLE-METHOD:** Select right-triangle ratios, Law of Sines, or Law of Cosines from the given data.

## Unit Circle and Exact-Value Skills

- **SK-TRIG-UNIT-CIRCLE-COORD:** Determine unit-circle coordinates for common angles.
- **SK-TRIG-SIGN-QUADRANT:** Determine the sign of a trigonometric function by quadrant.
- **SK-TRIG-RECIPROCAL:** Relate sine/cosecant, cosine/secant, and tangent/cotangent.
- **SK-TRIG-EXACT-VALUE:** Express common trigonometric values exactly.
- **SK-TRIG-CIRCLE-POINT:** Use coordinates and radius to evaluate trigonometric functions.

## Graphing and Modeling Skills

- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-VOCAB:** Recall and correctly use the terms amplitude, midline, period, phase shift, vertical shift, and asymptote.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-KEYPOINTS:** Locate quarter-period key points of a periodic function.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-AMPLITUDE:** Determine amplitude and vertical reflection.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-MIDLINE:** Determine a sinusoid's midline and vertical shift.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-PERIOD:** Determine period from a graph or equation.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-PHASE:** Determine horizontal shift from a graph or equation.
- **SK-TRIG-GRAPH-ASYMPTOTE:** Locate vertical asymptotes of tangent, secant, and cosecant.
- **SK-TRIG-MODEL-PARAMETERS:** Translate contextual maximum, minimum, cycle, and starting position into model parameters.

## Equation and Identity Skills

- **SK-TRIG-INVERSE-NOTATION:** Read inverse-trigonometric notation and distinguish it from reciprocal notation.
- **SK-TRIG-INVERSE-RANGE:** Recall the principal-value ranges of inverse trigonometric functions.
- **SK-TRIG-INVERSE-EVALUATE:** Evaluate an inverse trigonometric expression exactly or approximately.
- **SK-TRIG-EQUATION-ISOLATE:** Isolate a trigonometric expression before solving.
- **SK-TRIG-EQUATION-INTERVAL:** Use reference angles and periodicity to find every solution in an interval.
- **SK-TRIG-EQUATION-GENERAL:** Express a periodic solution family in general form.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-SUBSTITUTE:** Substitute an identity to rewrite an expression.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-STRATEGY:** Choose a productive identity or algebraic manipulation.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-VERIFY:** Maintain equivalence while transforming one side of an identity.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-RECIPROCAL-QUOTIENT:** Apply reciprocal and quotient identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-PYTHAGOREAN:** Apply the Pythagorean trigonometric identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-EVEN-ODD:** Apply even and odd trigonometric identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-COFUNCTION:** Apply sine/cosine cofunction identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-DOUBLE-ANGLE:** Apply double-angle identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-HALF-ANGLE:** Apply half-angle identities.
- **SK-TRIG-IDENTITY-SUM-DIFFERENCE:** Apply angle sum and difference identities.

## Polar, Vector, and Parametric Skills

- **SK-POL-NOTATION-READ:** Read and write polar-coordinate notation and identify the radius, angle, pole, and polar axis.
- **SK-VEC-NOTATION-VOCAB:** Read and write vector notation and correctly use the terms magnitude, direction, component, and unit vector.
- **SK-PAR-NOTATION-VOCAB:** Read parametric notation and correctly use the terms parameter, coordinate equation, orientation, and parameter interval.
- **SK-POL-PLOT:** Plot a point from radius and angle.
- **SK-POL-EQUIVALENT:** Generate equivalent polar coordinate pairs.
- **SK-POL-CONVERT-POINT:** Use coordinate relationships to convert a point between systems.
- **SK-POL-CONVERT-EQUATION:** Substitute coordinate identities to convert an equation between systems.
- **SK-VEC-COMPONENTS:** Write a vector in component form.
- **SK-VEC-OPERATE:** Perform component-wise vector operations.
- **SK-VEC-MAGNITUDE:** Calculate vector magnitude using the distance formula.
- **SK-VEC-DIRECTION:** Determine a vector's direction angle with correct quadrant.
- **SK-VEC-UNIT:** Normalize a nonzero vector to obtain a unit vector.
- **SK-VEC-3D:** Represent and operate on vectors with three components.
- **SK-PAR-TABLE:** Generate ordered pairs from parametric equations.
- **SK-PAR-GRAPH:** Graph a parametric curve with its direction of motion.
- **SK-PAR-ELIMINATE:** Eliminate a parameter algebraically.
- **SK-PAR-MODEL:** Interpret or construct parametric equations for planar motion.
