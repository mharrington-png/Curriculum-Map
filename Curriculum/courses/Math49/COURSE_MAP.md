# Middlesex Math Course Overview

## MATH 49

**Course title:** Precalculus with Limits

**Status:** Approved curriculum structure and objective-to-skill mapping.

## 1. Review Content

Review content supports entry into Math 49. Teachers should diagnose and reinforce it as needed rather than treat it with the same pacing or assessment priority as new required content.

- Domain and range from formulas, graphs, and tables
- Transformations of functions
- Exponential and logarithmic functions
- Review of trigonometric functions as needed

## 2. Required Content (Covered Every Semester)

### Unit 1: Function Operations, Composition, and Inverses

- Even and odd symmetry
- Sums, differences, products, and quotients of functions
- Composition and decomposition of functions
- Domain restrictions under function operations
- Inverse functions from formulas, graphs, and tables

### Unit 2: Power, Polynomial, and Rational Functions

- Power functions and proportionality/variation
- The algebraic and graphical Math 39 power-function sequence at greater depth, including fractional exponents
- Polynomial structure, zeros, multiplicity, and end behavior
- Rational functions, discontinuities, and asymptotes
- Modeling and comparison using function families

### Unit 3: Limits and Function Comparison

- Limit notation and meaning
- Limits from graphs, tables, and formulas
- Limits at infinity
- Infinite limits and asymptotic behavior
- Comparison of long-run growth among function families

### Unit 4: Sequences and Series

- Sequence notation and evaluation
- Explicit and recursive definitions
- Arithmetic and geometric sequences
- Modeling discrete change
- Finite and infinite series notation
- Arithmetic and geometric series
- Convergence of geometric series
- Applications of series and accumulated change

## 3. Extension Material (Optional/Time-Permitting)

- One-sided limits and existence of two-sided limits
- Function values, limits, and continuity at a point
- Parametric curves, parameter elimination, and planar-motion models
- Circles, ellipses, and hyperbolas from implicit, standard, and parametric equations
- Completing the square to convert general conic equations to standard form
- Centers, vertices, axes, foci, asymptotes, and distance-based geometric definitions
- Geometric definition of a parabola from its focus and directrix
- Complex numbers
- Vectors
- Polar coordinates and equations

## 4. Depth, Emphasis, and Pedagogical Notes

- Math 31 material should be reviewed through purposeful problems rather than retaught as isolated chapters.
- Function behavior should be connected across formulas, graphs, tables, and contexts.
- Composition, inverse, and domain questions should include non-formula representations.
- Limits require supplemental resources because the mapped OpenStax section does not cover the full intended range of graphical, tabular, and algebraic reasoning.
- Sequences and series should include modeling and applications, not only symbolic manipulation.
- Department notation should remain consistent across courses even when textbook notation differs.
- OpenStax is a resource mapped to the curriculum; it does not determine the objectives or unit order.
