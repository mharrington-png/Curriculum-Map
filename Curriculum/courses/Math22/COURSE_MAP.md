# Middlesex Math Course Overview

## MATH 22

**Course title:** Geometry

**Status:** Draft extracted from the Fall 2025 Math 22 Geometry packet; requires faculty review.

## 1. Review Content

Review content supports geometric problem solving and should be diagnosed and reinforced as needed.

- Solving linear equations
- Evaluating and simplifying expressions
- Ratios, proportions, and scale factors
- Square roots and the Pythagorean theorem
- Coordinate-plane distance, midpoint, and slope

## 2. Required Content (Covered Every Semester)

### Unit 1: Geometry Basics

- Points, lines, planes, segments, rays, and geometric notation
- Segment measurement, betweenness, addition, midpoint, and bisectors
- Angle measurement, addition, bisectors, and angle pairs
- Logical reasoning with postulates, theorems, and counterexamples
- Parallel and perpendicular lines
- Angle relationships formed by transversals
- Converses and introductory proof

### Unit 2: Triangle Congruence and Proof

- Triangle classification and angle relationships
- Polygon and triangle congruence notation
- Triangle congruence by SAS, SSS, HL, ASA, and AAS
- Isosceles and equilateral triangle theorems and converses
- CPCTC and structured geometric proof

### Unit 3: Quadrilaterals, Perimeter, and Area

- Interior and exterior angles of polygons
- Perimeter and area of polygons
- Properties and tests for parallelograms
- Rhombi, rectangles, and squares
- Trapezoids and midsegments
- Kites and composite polygons
- Proofs involving quadrilateral properties

### Unit 4: Similarity and Proportional Reasoning

- Dilations and scale factors
- Similar polygons
- Perimeter and area relationships under similarity
- Triangle similarity by AA, SSS, and SAS
- Triangle proportionality theorems and converses
- Proofs involving similarity

### Unit 5: Right Triangles

- Pythagorean theorem and its converse
- Triangle inequality relationships
- Special right triangles
- Right-triangle similarity and geometric-mean relationships
- Sine, cosine, and tangent
- Solving right triangles and applications

### Unit 6: Circles

- Circle vocabulary and notation
- Circumference and arc length
- Area of circles, sectors, and segments
- Central, inscribed, and exterior angle relationships
- Chord relationships
- Tangent and secant relationships
- Segment-length relationships involving chords, tangents, and secants

## 3. Extension Material (Optional/Time-Permitting)

None. All six listed units are required every semester.

## 4. Depth, Emphasis, and Pedagogical Notes

- Require students to read, name, and mark diagrams accurately before calculating.
- Treat definitions, postulates, theorems, converses, and proof as recurring practices rather than an isolated proof unit.
- Connect algebraic equation solving to segment, angle, perimeter, area, similarity, and circle relationships.
- Require correct congruence and similarity correspondence order.
- Balance numerical calculation with justification of the theorem or relationship used.
- Maintain exact values when appropriate and distinguish exact from measured or rounded results.
- The packet is a resource attached to the curriculum; its page order does not permanently own the objectives.
