# Middlesex Math Course Overview

## MATH 12

**Course title:** Intermediate Algebra

**Status:** Draft normalized from the existing Math 12 course map; requires faculty review.

## 1. Review Content

Review content supports entry into Math 12. Teachers should diagnose and reinforce it as needed rather than treat it with the same pacing or assessment priority as new required content.

- Prime factorization
- Order of operations
- Combining like terms
- Operations with integers and fractions
- Distributive property

## 2. Required Content (Covered Every Semester)

### Unit 1: Linear Equations and Applications

- Simplifying and solving linear equations in multiple forms
- Equations containing fractions
- Literal equations and isolating a variable
- Number, mixture, and motion problems
- Organized mathematical presentation

### Unit 2: Linear Inequalities and Absolute-Value Equations

- One-variable linear inequalities
- Compound inequalities using AND and OR
- Interval notation and graphical representations
- Absolute-value equations

### Unit 3: Lines and Introductory Functions

- Graphing lines from common equation forms
- Slope and rates of change
- Parallel and perpendicular lines
- Determining equations of lines
- Function notation and input/output
- Vertical line test
- Domain and range from graphs, tables, and contexts

### Unit 4: Systems of Linear Equations

- Intersections as solutions to systems
- Solving two-variable systems by graphing, substitution, and elimination
- Applications involving two-variable systems

### Unit 5: Polynomial Operations and Basic Factoring

- Exponent rules
- Adding and subtracting polynomials
- Multiplying polynomials
- Dividing a polynomial by a monomial
- Factoring out a greatest common factor

## 3. Extension Material (Optional/Time-Permitting)

- Absolute-value inequalities
- Systems of linear inequalities
- Factoring trinomials
- Discrete and continuous relationships

## 4. Depth, Emphasis, and Pedagogical Notes

- Require clear, organized algebraic work, especially when solving equations and applications.
- Emphasize interval notation for inequalities.
- Keep functions introductory but conceptually strong enough to support Math 21.
- Teach domain and range primarily from graphs, tables, and contexts rather than difficult formulas.
- Emphasize the connection between a system's solution and the intersection of graphs.
- Restrict systems to two variables.
- OpenStax function coverage may require supplementation, including resources from the Math 21 workbook.
- OpenStax is a resource mapped to the curriculum; it does not determine the objectives or unit order.
