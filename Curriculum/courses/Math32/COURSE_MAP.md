# Middlesex Math Course Overview

## MATH 32

**Course title:** Trigonometry Fundamentals

**Status:** Draft extracted from the 2025 Math 32 workbook; requires faculty review.

## 1. Critical Content (Covered Every Semester)

### Unit 1: Trigonometric Foundations

- Right-triangle relationships, special right triangles, and the Pythagorean theorem
- Degree and radian measure, coterminal angles, reference angles, and arc length
- Unit-circle coordinates and exact values of all six trigonometric functions
- Right-triangle and coordinate-based applications
- Multiple solutions to basic trigonometric equations

### Unit 2: Periodic Functions and Modeling

- Graphs of sine and cosine
- Amplitude, midline, period, phase shift, and reflection
- Equations from graphs and graphs from equations
- Periodic models in context
- Graphs of tangent, secant, and cosecant, including asymptotes

### Unit 3: Inverse Trigonometric Functions and Equations

- Principal-value ranges of inverse sine, cosine, and tangent
- Exact and approximate evaluation of inverse trigonometric functions
- Trigonometric equations on restricted intervals and in general form
- Compositions of trigonometric and inverse trigonometric functions

### Unit 4: Non-Right Triangles

- Law of Sines, including the ambiguous SSA case
- Law of Cosines
- Selection and application of an appropriate triangle-solving method

### Unit 5: Trigonometric Identities

- Reciprocal and quotient identities, including `tan(theta) = sin(theta)/cos(theta)`
- Pythagorean identities
- Even/odd and cofunction identities
- Algebraic verification using the required identity families

### Unit 6: Polar Coordinates

- Plotting and identifying polar points
- Equivalent polar coordinate pairs
- Conversion of points between polar and rectangular coordinates

## 2. Extension Material (Optional/Time-Permitting)

- Graphing and interpreting polar functions
- Conversion between polar and rectangular equations
- Two- and three-dimensional vectors
- Parametric equations and elimination of the parameter
- Triangle area using two sides and an included angle
- Double-angle, half-angle, and angle sum/difference identities
- Exact-value evaluation using extension identities

Additional identities, triangle area from SAS, polar functions, vectors, and parametrics are extension material. Polar coordinates of points and inverse-trigonometric evaluation are required.

## 3. Depth, Emphasis, and Pedagogical Notes

- Exact values and structural reasoning should precede calculator approximation.
- Students should connect right-triangle, unit-circle, graphical, and functional interpretations of trigonometry.
- Graphing should emphasize parameter meaning and model interpretation, not only point plotting.
- Equation solving should distinguish a principal calculator output from all solutions in an interval or in general form.
- Triangle applications should require students to identify the available information and justify their choice of method.
- Identity work should emphasize purposeful substitution and algebraic equivalence rather than memorization alone.
- Objectives are independent of this workbook. Workbook pages are resource mappings and may be replaced.
