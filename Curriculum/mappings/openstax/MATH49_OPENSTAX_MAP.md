# Math 49 OpenStax Mapping

This draft translates the existing Math 49 course map into an objective-centered resource crosswalk. OpenStax sections are replaceable resources; the Math 49 objectives remain authoritative.

## Resource Record and Alignment Labels

- **Resource:** OpenStax *Precalculus 2e* (Jay Abramson, 2021)
- **Mapping level:** Textbook section to learning objective; examples and exercises are not yet mapped
- **Direct:** Explicitly teaches the LO
- **Partial:** Teaches only part of the LO or needs careful selection
- **Supporting:** Supplies prerequisite or adjacent content
- **Gap:** No direct match in the currently approved section range

## Teacher Supplements Needed

| Objective(s) | Supplement needed | Why the textbook map is insufficient |
|---|---|---|
| M49-FND-003 | Even/odd symmetry lesson and practice | Symmetry is required in Math 49 but is not identified in the mapped function-review sections. |
| M49-OPS-002; M49-OPS-007 | Composition and inverse interpretation from tables | Section 1.4 includes some table work, but teachers should add enough table-based practice to make it an assessed representation. |
| M49-PWR-002 through M49-PWR-005; M49-PWR-008 | Fractional-power behavior and power-model construction | Sections 3.3, 3.8, and 3.9 must be combined; no single section supplies the complete progression. |
| M49-PWR-006; M49-LIM-007 | Long-run function-family comparison | Teachers need a synthesis resource comparing logarithmic, power, polynomial, rational, and exponential growth on common scales. |
| M49-ALG-008 | Model-selection tasks | The textbook supports variation directly, but polynomial and rational model choice needs additional contextual datasets or scenarios. |
| M49-LIM-003 | Algebraic limit evaluation | Add OpenStax 12.2 or equivalent work on substitution, factoring, cancellation, and rationalization. |
| M49-LIM-005; M49-LIM-006 | Infinite limits and limits at infinity | Section 12.1 does not cover these required outcomes at the intended depth. |
| M49-LIM-008 | Continuity at a point | Add OpenStax 12.3 or equivalent work distinguishing function value, limit, and continuity. |
| M49-SEQ-001; M49-SEQ-005 | Sequence graphs and discrete-change models | The core sequence sections need graphing and contextual modeling tasks added explicitly. |
| M49-SER-005 | Accumulated-change series models | Add applications such as repeated deposits, total distance, or accumulated quantities; section 11.4 alone is too generic. |
| M49-PAR-003 | Parametric motion modeling | Add problems interpreting time, direction, and component equations in plane motion. |
| M49-CON-003 | Circle equations and graphs | The current 10.1-10.3 conic range omits a complete circle treatment. |
| M49-CON-004; M49-CON-005 | Parametric ellipse and hyperbola representations | Coordinate with sections 8.6-8.7 or provide a targeted supplement. |
| M49-CON-001 through M49-CON-008 | Coherent conic overview and depth guidance | The textbook separates conic families; teachers need a common comparison sheet and an agreed boundary for required features, completing the square, focal definitions, and parametric forms. |
