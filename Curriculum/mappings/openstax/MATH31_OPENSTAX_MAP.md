# Math 31 OpenStax Mapping

This draft translates the existing Math 31 transition notes into an objective-centered crosswalk for OpenStax College Algebra. OpenStax sections are replaceable resources; the Math 31 objectives remain authoritative.

## Resource Record and Alignment Labels

- **Resource:** OpenStax *College Algebra 2e* (Jay Abramson, 2021)
- **Mapping level:** Textbook section to learning objective; examples and exercises are not yet mapped
- **Direct:** Explicitly teaches the LO
- **Partial:** Teaches only part of the LO or needs careful selection
- **Supporting:** Supplies prerequisite or adjacent content
- **Gap:** No direct match in the currently identified sections

## Teacher Supplements Needed

| Objective(s) | Supplement needed | Why the textbook map is insufficient |
|---|---|---|
| M31-REV-001 through M31-REV-004 | Common prerequisite diagnostics | Percentage, exponent, polynomial-vocabulary, and binomial-expansion review should be diagnosed rather than retaught as core units. |
| M31-LIN-003; M31-FUN-002; M31-FUN-003 | Function evaluation and domain/range from tables | The LOs require balanced use of formulas, graphs, and tables. |
| M31-LIN-007; M31-LIN-008 | Total-change and units tasks | Teachers need problems that explicitly connect average rate, interval length, total change, and compound units. |
| M31-FUN-005; M31-FUN-006 | Piecewise graph/formula translation set | Section 3.2 introduces piecewise functions but does not provide enough two-way translation at the stated depth. |
| M31-FUN-008 | Transformation effects on asymptotes | General transformations need examples drawn from rational, exponential, and logarithmic families. |
| M31-FUN-009; M31-FUN-012; M31-FUN-017 | Composition and inverses from graphs and tables, with meaning and units | Section 3.4 and 3.7 are strongest algebraically; the course requires interpretation across representations. |
| M31-FUN-013 through M31-FUN-015 | Inverse units and domain/range reasoning | These interpretation outcomes are more explicit and deeper than the textbook treatment. |
| M31-FUN-018 | Concavity from changing average rate of change | A purpose-built graph/table activity is required. |
| M31-QUAD-006; M31-QUAD-007 | Quadratic construction and trajectory modeling | Select or supply tasks built from zeros, vertices, graphs, and physical constraints. |
| M31-EXP-001; M31-EXP-003; M31-EXP-007; M31-EXP-009 | Exponential comparison across tables, graphs, formulas, and data | The course expects more cross-representation construction and comparison than a single section sequence provides. |
| M31-EXP-010; M31-LOG-011 | Discrete/continuous exponential conversion and continuous percent change | Explicit instruction is needed to connect forms such as `ab^x` and `ae^{kx}`. |
| M31-LOG-010 | Intersections of two exponential functions | Add graphical and algebraic comparison tasks rather than only isolated equation exercises. |
| M31-EXT-002 | Even/odd symmetry resource | Not identified in the mapped core sections. |
| M31-EXT-003 | Contextual logarithmic-scale applications | Add examples such as pH, sound intensity, or earthquake magnitude at an appropriate depth. |
