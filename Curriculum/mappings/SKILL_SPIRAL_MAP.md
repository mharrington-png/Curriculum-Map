# Cross-Course Supporting Skill Spiral

Supporting skills recur by design. A skill may first appear as an explicit lesson target, later function as review, and eventually become an assumed tool inside a more advanced objective.

## Progression Labels

- **I - Introduce:** First sustained instruction and guided practice
- **R - Reinforce:** Revisit for fluency or repair misconceptions
- **D - Deepen:** Add representations, abstraction, interpretation, or complexity
- **A - Apply:** Use as a supporting tool inside a new topic

These labels describe the role of a skill in a course, not whether the course content is Review, Required, or Extension.

## High-Frequency Canonical Skill Families

### Equation Solving

Introduced in Math 12 through `SK-ALG-INVERSE-OPERATIONS`, `SK-ALG-VARIABLE-ISOLATE`, and `SK-ALG-CHECK-SOLUTION`. It returns in:

- Math 21 rational, radical, and quadratic equations
- Math 22 segment, angle, similarity, and circle equations
- Math 31 exponential and logarithmic equations
- Math 32 trigonometric equations
- Math 49 limit simplification and advanced function analysis

### Function Representation

Begins with `SK-FUNC-NOTATION-READ`, graph/table domain and range, and linear graphs in Math 12. It spirals through:

- Math 21 comparison among formulas, graphs, tables, and contexts
- Math 31 piecewise functions, transformations, composition, inverses, and concavity
- Math 32 periodic graphs and models
- Math 49 long-run behavior, limits, sequences, and advanced composition

### Factoring and Algebraic Structure

GCF and distribution begin in Math 12. Math 21 develops trinomial and special-case factoring. The same skills later support:

- Quadratic analysis in Math 31
- Exact simplification and trigonometric equations in Math 32
- Polynomial zeros, rational discontinuities, and limits in Math 49

### Proportional and Geometric Reasoning

Equation and ratio skills from Math 12 and Math 21 support Math 22 similarity. Math 22 then supplies `SK-TRI-PYTHAGOREAN`, `SK-TRI-SPECIAL-RATIOS`, and `SK-TRI-TRIG-RATIO`, which Math 32 deepens into unit-circle, non-right-triangle, and identity work.

### Transformations, Inverses, and Structure

Function transformations begin informally in Math 21, become explicit and representational in Math 31, support periodic graphing in Math 32, and return in Math 49 as assumed tools for advanced function comparison and limits.

## Interpretation Guidance

- A recurring skill is not unnecessary repetition; the representation, independence, or conceptual demand should increase.
- A course may list a skill as Review while still deepening it inside a required unit.
- Later courses should reference the original canonical skill ID unless they introduce a genuinely new, smaller supporting ability.
- Course objectives describe what students should accomplish in that course. Supporting skill IDs describe the reusable tools that make those outcomes possible.
